DIABETES PREVENTION GUIDELINES

Primary Prevention Strategies:
1. Lifestyle Modification
   - Weight loss: 5-7% of body weight can reduce diabetes risk by 58%
   - Physical activity: 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity exercise
   - Dietary changes: Reduce refined carbohydrates, increase fiber intake
   - Smoking cessation: Smoking increases insulin resistance

2. Dietary Recommendations
   - Mediterranean diet pattern shown to reduce diabetes incidence by 52%
   - Low glycemic index foods help maintain stable blood glucose
   - Portion control using 9-inch plates
   - Limit sugary beverages to <1 serving per week
   - Increase vegetables to 5+ servings daily

3. Physical Activity Guidelines
   - Aerobic exercise: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity (brisk walking, cycling)
   - Resistance training: 2 sessions per week
   - Reduce sedentary time: Stand up every 30 minutes
   - Exercise improves insulin sensitivity for 24-48 hours post-activity

4. Weight Management
   - BMI target: 18.5-24.9 kg/m²
   - Waist circumference: <35 inches women, <40 inches men
   - Even modest weight loss (5-10%) significantly improves insulin sensitivity
   - Gradual weight loss (1-2 lbs/week) more sustainable than rapid loss

5. Monitoring and Screening
   - Fasting glucose check every 3 years if normal risk
   - Annual screening if prediabetic (A1C 5.7-6.4%)
   - Blood pressure monitoring: <130/80 mmHg target
   - Lipid panel annually: LDL <100 mg/dL target

Evidence Base:
- Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP): Lifestyle intervention reduced diabetes incidence by 58% vs metformin (31%)
- Finnish Diabetes Prevention Study: 5% weight loss + exercise reduced risk by 58%
- Look AHEAD trial: Intensive lifestyle intervention improved A1C, weight, and fitness

Risk Factors:
- Age >45 years
- BMI >25 kg/m²
- Family history of diabetes
- Physical inactivity
- History of gestational diabetes
- Hypertension (>140/90 mmHg)
- Dyslipidemia (HDL <35, TG >250)
- Prediabetes (impaired fasting glucose or A1C 5.7-6.4%)

Warning Signs:
- Increased thirst
- Frequent urination
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing sores
- Frequent infections

Medication Considerations:
- Metformin first-line for high-risk prediabetes
- Consider GLP-1 agonists for obese patients with prediabetes
- Statin therapy for cardiovascular risk reduction
- ACE inhibitors for hypertension in diabetic patients

Follow-up Schedule:
- Initial visit: Comprehensive assessment
- 3-month follow-up: Check progress, adjust plan
- 6-month follow-up: Reassess risk factors
- Annual: Full metabolic panel, complications screening

Complications Prevention:
- Annual eye exams (retinopathy screening)
- Annual urine microalbumin (nephropathy screening)
- Annual comprehensive foot exam (neuropathy screening)
- Cardiovascular risk assessment every visit
